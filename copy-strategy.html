Foundation

Copy Strategy - Nick Schiffer

Foundation doc · Voice, brand pillars, headline bank, framing AG1 will write to.

Voice

Craft-led, operator-to-operator, anti-hype. Nick's voice is grounded in a decade of actual custom builds - not marketing theory. The tone respects the trade, respects the client, and respects the complexity of a $1M+ custom home project. No guru-speak, no hustle-porn, no empty motivational language. Every sentence earns its place by being specific and useful.

Use: builder, operator, the build, the process, the craft, the project, the client, systems, install, execute, the work, hold the standard, the Modern Craftsman way Avoid: game-changer, revolutionary, unlock, level up, hustle harder, mindset, abundance, secrets, hacks, crush it, 10x, grind

Brand Pillars

  1. Modern Craftsman - the philosophy that craft-led building is a competitive advantage, not a cost center. Every deliverable reflects that quality thinking and quality execution are the same thing.
  2. Process-Led - NS Builders' reputation is built on repeatable, documented systems. The course teaches the system, not the inspiration.
  3. Trade-Respecting - the audience is real builders with real projects. Copy talks to them as professionals, not students.
  4. Proof-by-Build - credentials come from the actual work (decade of Eastern MA luxury builds, the Modern Craftsman audience built through content, not paid ads). Never overclaim.

Headline Bank

For the Custom Builder's Playbook landing page, hub callouts, and ad creative:

  • Build a custom home business that doesn't eat your weekends.
  • The NS Builders process - translated into systems for other builders.
  • Craft-led. Process-driven. The Modern Craftsman operator's playbook.
  • Six modules. Built by a luxury GC. For $1M-$5M custom builders.
  • From job-site content to a productised system - finally.
  • What I'd run if I were starting NS Builders from scratch today.
  • The custom build process other builders keep asking for - now installable.
  • Client systems, estimating, project management, subs, brand, scale. In that order.

Framing for the Funnel

  • Top of funnel (scorecard, IG hooks, podcast CTAs): operator-pain led. "Your estimating is leaking margin, your sub relationships are transactional, your Fridays are firefighting" - real-build hooks beat lifestyle hooks.
  • Mid funnel (course landing, VSL): Modern Craftsman as the bridge. The content got them following; the Playbook gets them building differently.
  • High funnel (mastermind application): operator-to-operator framing, craft-community ethos, "if the Playbook isn't enough, this is the next room."

Banned Phrases

Universal AG1 ban list, plus Nick-specific: - No "blueprint" (overused in construction-coach niche) - No "secrets" or "hacks" - No "unlock" - No "transform" unless paired with a specific before/after metric - No revenue-guarantee language on cold outreach - No first-month-lift language without NDA caveat context

Tone Calibration By Surface

Surface Register Length
IG hook Direct, specific to a build problem 1 line
Ad copy Operator-led, claim + qualifier 2-3 lines
Email subject Question or curiosity, no clickbait 5-9 words
VSL opening "I've been building custom homes in Eastern MA since 2014." 1 sentence cold open
Landing hero What's at stake for a $1M-$5M custom builder 1 promise + 1 mechanism
FAQ Plain answers, no hedge 2-3 sentences each

Voice Test

Every paragraph passes if a $2M custom builder reading it nods and thinks "that's exactly the problem." If it could appear in a generic "scale your business" online course, rewrite it until it couldn't.
